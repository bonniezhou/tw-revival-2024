Tweedle

Rina Alfonso

Tweedle is a revival typeface based on the scotch roman style type used found in a 1909 edition of Lewis Carroll’s “Through the Looking-Glass and What Alice Found There.” Throughout the term, I worked to make the type feel more evenly textured with less contrast for increased legibility. Proportions were adjusted for better spacing and texture in comparison to the original model, while some quirky design details were preserved in order to maintain the personality of the original type that speaks to the source and material’s essence. Tweedle is intended for text use and in applications that need a friendly, readable typeface that combines classic modernist sensibilities with playfulness.

Rina is a multidisciplinary designer, artist, and educator originally from the Philippines and based in Washington, DC. She is the founder and creative director of Studio Aorta, a boutique creative agency producing exhibitions, print design, and illustrations, and an adjunct associate professor at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Her extensive work with type in her projects for compelling visualizations led her to delve into type design. Prior to Type West, Rina graduated from the Type@Cooper 2023 Condensed program. Through Type West, she hopes to continue broadening her type design skills and deepen her knowledge of type, further enriching her art and design practice.
