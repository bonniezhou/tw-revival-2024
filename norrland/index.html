Norrland

Kim-Kim Bui

Norrland is a contemporary revival of Nordisk Antikva (Genzsch Antiqua), which was originally released around 1906. The original typeface was designed by the Swedish printer Waldemar Zachrisson and the German type designer Friedrich Bauer. Norrland is a text typeface with a calligraphic influence, adapted to today’s digital requirements. The aim was to capture and preserve the personality of Nordisk Antikva while applying features that improve legibility, such as adding straight stem and proportional letterforms. Norrland (meaning Northland) refers to the northern part of Sweden, which covers around two thirds of the country’s surface.

Kim-Kim is a graphic designer based in Sweden. Her design work focuses on book design, sensory design and packaging design. With a background in cognitive neuroscience and marketing, she believes that design should be accessible and inclusive. Her design work combines the digital tools with craftsmanship and explores ways to awaken the senses.
