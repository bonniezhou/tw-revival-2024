Type Revivals

Wicked

Aasawari S. Kulkarni

Wicked is a revival of the typeface used in the first edition of The Wonderful Wizard of the Oz. The reference material was found in the Library of Congress, Rare Book and Special Collections Division. The original book was written and illustrated by L Frank Baum and William Wallace Denslo; printed by Messrs. A. R. Barnes & Company. The original typeface used was Monotype Old Style.

The short ascenders and descenders, flat shoulders, big apertures, and the slightly diagonal axes from the original face was a big source of inspiration for the revival. Wicked, while keeping with the softness and fluidity of the original face, takes liberties in the stroke widths and terminals to make serifs sharper and contrast them with ball terminals. With this revival, Wicked tries to keep up with the magic and gentleness of the original pages of the book.

Aasawari Kulkarni is a designer, educator, and writer originally from India. Currently she serves as Assistant Professor of Design at Corcoran School of the Arts and Design at the George Washington University in Washington D.C.
