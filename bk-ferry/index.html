Type Revivals

Bk Ferry

Tim Cronin

It didn’t take me long to decide that Walt Whitman’s “Leaves of Grass” would be the source material for my revival typeface. I knew I wanted something I could connect with, close to my heart and — as is the case with Whitman's celebrated volume — close to my home. “Leaves of Grass” was published in Brooklyn during a pivotal era in queer history. In fact, the first edition was printed just blocks away from my neighborhood, at the Rome Brothers print shop in Brooklyn Heights in 1855. We know from the text itself that Whitman frequented this area, and the docks around the Fulton Street ferry in particular, in the years leading up to the construction of the Brooklyn Bridge. Within the earliest editions of the book lives a clandestine homoeroticism that provides a beautifully unique and honest perspective on this moment in Brooklyn history.

Importantly, Whitman himself set many of the pages by hand, and drew a beautifully embellished version of the body type for the cover. The printing is inky and inconsistent, lending a unique character to this edition. I decided to embrace some of this imperfect quality in my revival, making for a fun and challenging project.

Tim Cronin is a graphic designer and printmaker living in Brooklyn, New York. He currently works at WaitWhat, a media invention company, as the VP of Design. Tim has over a decade of design experience in agency, in-house, and freelance capacities, and taught graphic design and printmaking as a visiting artist at the high school level for two years. He graduated with a Master's degree in Graphic and Web Design from the Minneapolis College of Art and Design in 2019, and he is currently enrolled in Type West at the Letterform archive.
